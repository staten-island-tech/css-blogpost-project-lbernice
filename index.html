Orchids that Resemble Animals

1. Monkey Orchids

Monkey Orchids, also known as the Dracula simia, is native to the tropical highland forests of Southeastern Ecuador. The name translates to "little dragon monkey." Monkey Orchids could represent evil and death, as well as power and authority. What makes this particular flower captivating is its resemblance to a monkey's face, which is located at the base of the petals. Monkey Orchids may bloom at any time and give off a scent of ripe oranges.

2. White Egret Orchids

White Egret Orchids, also known as the Pecteillis Radiata, is native to Japan. It is known for its beautiful appearance and resemblance to a white egret (a bird) in flight. White Egret flowers typically represent innocence, purity, and grace. They are also easy to care for. Despite how easy it is to cultivate these flowers, it remains to be an endangered species.

3. Flying Duck Orchids

Flying Duck Orchids, also known as the Caleana major, is native to the Australian wilderness. These orchids are so unique due to their duck-like blooms. Flying Duck Orchids may only survive with the presence of fungus. Likewise to the White Egret Orchid, it is also classified as an endangered species.
